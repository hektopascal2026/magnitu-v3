I. What is Magnitu?

A personal relevance engine that learns what you care about.

Magnitu scores every incoming article, email, and legal notice on a scale from 0 to 100 based on your priorities. It watches your judgments—as you read entries and assign them one of four labels, Magnitu builds a model to predict your judgment on entries you haven't seen yet.

The Four Labels

Investigation Lead The highest-value category. Points to a story worth pursuing, evidence of wrongdoing, or connections between actors.
Important Relevant to your beat and worth reading, but not an actionable lead on its own.
Background Tangentially related or good to know, but not something you'd act on today.
Noise Irrelevant to your work, such as press releases, off-topic content, or duplicates.

II. Architecture & Workspaces

Designed for newsrooms where teams share a data pool but have diverging beats.

  • The Mothership: All profiles share a single, global connection to the main Seismo instance. You sync entries once, and embedding computation runs once per entry for all profiles.
  • Multi-Profile Architecture: One Magnitu instance can run multiple isolated profiles. Each profile trains its own model on its own labels and manages its own recipe.
  • Lightweight Satellites: Each profile can push its specific scores to a dedicated "satellite" Seismo installation (e.g., one PHP install per topic). The satellite maintains its own database for Magnitu scores and state, while the raw entries stream from the mothership.
  • Sharing (.magnitu files): Models can be exported as a zip archive containing the manifest.json, labels.json, model.joblib, calibration.json, and recipe.json.
  • Forking: You can "fork" a team's base model to inherit their shared baseline judgments (e.g., ignoring general press releases), which you can then customize for your specific beat.

IV. Workflow & Strategy

How you interact with Magnitu and guide its learning.

The Learning Loop

Magnitu improves through a repeating five-step cycle. Each cycle sharpens the model's accuracy.

1

Sync

Pull new entries from the main Seismo feed aggregator. Run a standard "Sync" for quick incremental updates or a "Full Sync" for source-by-source backfill and embedding completion.

2

Label

Read entries and assign them one of the four categories. Each label serves as a concrete training example.

3

Train

Magnitu builds a completely new model from all your historical labels. You need at least {{ config.min_labels_to_train or 20 }} labels to start.

4

Score & Push

The model scores every entry and sends those numbers, along with a lightweight keyword "recipe", to Seismo.

5

Review & Correct

Check the Top 30 page to review the model's highest-ranked entries. Reclassifying errors targets exact areas of model confusion and yields the highest-value training data.

Advanced Labeling Strategies

The Smart Queue

Magnitu mixes several strategies to surface entries that will improve the model fastest:

Uncertain

Entries where the model's probabilities are evenly spread.

Conflict

Entries where the deep local model and the lightweight PHP recipe disagree.

Diverse

Entries from underrepresented source categories to broaden the model's experience.

New

The most recent unlabeled entries.

Reasoning Notes & Legal Patterns

Reasoning Notes: The deep model does not read your note text directly. Instead, explaining a label lets the recipe distiller extract your matching keywords and boost them in the Seismo recipe. If enabled in settings, it also applies a Reasoning-Weight Boost to that row's training weight.

Legislation Monitoring: For tracking foreign legislation (e.g., Swiss impacts from EU law), Magnitu learns to spot hidden impacts by recognizing patterns across languages:

  • Exclusion language: "EU or EEA", "Binnenmarkt"
  • Third-country provisions: "Drittstaaten", "equivalence"
  • Compliance triggers: "CE-Kennzeichnung", "Konformitätsbewertung"

Workflow: Use the "Legislation" source filter to label 50–100 legal items, adding reasoning notes to boost terms. Check the Learned Legal Patterns panel on the Model page to ensure correct signals are picked up.

V. Under the Hood

The mechanics of how Magnitu understands text, trains models, and calculates scores.

How Magnitu Reads Text

Language Model: Magnitu uses a pre-trained transformer (default {{ config.transformer_model_name or 'xlm-roberta-base' }}). With 278 million parameters trained across 100 languages, it naturally understands what words mean—allowing terms like "Untersuchung" and "investigation" to produce similar vectors.

Meaning Vectors: Each article is converted into a 768-dimensional fingerprint based on its title (emphasized by repeating it once), description, a 500-character body excerpt, and structured Seismo context (source_type, source_name, source_category). Embedding runs once per article during sync and is cached.

Training & Distillation

The Classifier Head: Every time you train, Magnitu builds a Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) from scratch on your cached transformer embeddings. The language model provides the semantic understanding, while the MLP learns your priorities.

Knowledge Distillation: Because Seismo runs on PHP, it cannot execute Python transformer models. To bridge this, Magnitu trains a simpler TF-IDF student model to mimic the transformer's predictions, extracting a lightweight JSON "recipe" of weighted features (single words, bigrams, trigrams). This recipe is pushed to Seismo for millisecond live scoring.

Relevance Scores & Measuring Quality

Every entry gets a relevance score from 0 to 1 (shown as a percentage). Probabilities are calibrated using a temperature fit on a held-out slice of data to prevent overconfidence.

The final score is a weighted composite of calibrated probabilities:

  • Investigation Lead: Weight 1.0
  • Important: Weight 0.66
  • Background: Weight 0.33
  • Noise: Weight 0.0

Measuring Quality

Magnitu holds back ~20% of your labels as a test set. It calculates Accuracy and an F1 Score, which is crucial because it penalizes models that inflate accuracy by simply calling everything "noise".

Known Biases & Limitations

Text Length Keyword weights in the recipe are normalized to compensate. Local transformer body excerpts are capped at 500 characters.
Source Bias The recipe includes source weights (e.g. RSS toward noise). The deep model ignores source types unless they use distinctive language.
Class Imbalance Rare classes (like leads) are automatically up-weighted during training to prevent them from being ignored.
Recency Bias Unless Label Time-Decay is enabled, all labels hold equal weight regardless of age.

VI. Configuration

Settings are divided into global (shared) and per-profile knobs.

Profile Settings (Saved Per Profile)

Minimum Labels to Train Default 20. The safety gate before a model can generalize.
Recipe Top Keywords Default 200. Number of words/phrases pushed to the Seismo recipe.
Alert Threshold Default 0.75. The score above which Seismo highlights an entry as an alert.
Lead Discovery Blend Default 0, Max 0.25. Mixes extra investigation_lead probability into the final relevance score.
Label Time-Decay Default Off. Sets a half-life in days so older labels count less during training.
Reasoning-Weight Boost Default Off. Assigns a heavier training multiplier to labels with reasoning.

Global Settings (Shared Across All Profiles)

Legal-Signal Patterns: A user-configured list of regex/phrases. Matches are prepended to the embedded text as signals=... and aggressively boosted in the Seismo recipe. Changing this list invalidates all cached embeddings globally.
